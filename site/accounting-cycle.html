Step 1

Analyze Business Transactions

Every transaction must be analyzed to determine which accounts are affected and how (debit or credit). This is the foundation of double-entry bookkeeping.

💡
The Accounting Equation: Assets = Liabilities + Equity. Assets & Expenses increase with Debits. Liabilities, Equity & Revenue increase with Credits. Every entry must keep this equation in balance.

Post to the General Ledger

Each journal entry is posted to the General Ledger — a collection of T-accounts showing the running balance for each account. Debits on the left, credits on the right.

📖
T-accounts show debits on the left and credits on the right. Balance side: Assets/Expenses/Dividends = Debit normal balance • Liabilities/Equity/Revenue = Credit normal balance.

Step 5

Adjusting Entries

Adjusting entries record revenues earned or expenses incurred that haven't been journalized. They apply the Matching Principle — matching revenues to the period earned and expenses to the period incurred.

⚠️
Types of Adjustments: (1) Deferrals — prepaid expenses & unearned revenues already recorded but not yet earned/incurred. (2) Accruals — revenues earned or expenses incurred but not yet recorded. Every adjusting entry touches one Balance Sheet and one Income Statement account.

Step 8

Closing Entries & Post-Closing Trial Balance

Closing entries transfer balances from temporary accounts (revenues, expenses, dividends) into Retained Earnings, resetting them to zero for the next period.

✅
Temporary vs. Permanent Accounts: Temporary accounts (Revenue, Expenses, Dividends, Income Summary) are closed each period. Permanent accounts (Assets, Liabilities, Stockholders’ Equity) carry forward their balances. The four-step closing process: (1) Close revenues, (2) Close expenses, (3) Close Income Summary, (4) Close Dividends.
